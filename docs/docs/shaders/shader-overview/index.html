Shaders Overview

What is a shader?

A shader program is a compiled computer program executed on your graphics card that instructs the GPU "where" on the screen to draw to, and "what" to draw when it gets there. In the case of Indigo and WebGL, a shader is made up of a pair of programs, called the vertex (where) and fragment (what) programs. Other systems also support things like compute and geometry shaders, and we may be able to adopt those thing once WebGPU arrives.

The terms "shader", "program", and "shader program" are used somewhat inter-changeably.
